IPRoyal vs Decodo for Google AI Overview Scraping — Decision Report
Residential proxy due diligence (migrating away from Bright Data)
================================================================================


1. BOTTOM LINE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Neither provider repeats the Bright Data trap. Both IPRoyal and Decodo permit
google.com/search on raw rotating residential proxies, neither KYC-gates Google,
neither has an "immediate-vs-full access" tier that blocks search engines, both do
clean HTTPS passthrough (no custom CA cert, no SEC_ERROR_UNKNOWN_ISSUER), and
neither forces you onto a paid SERP API — the API is a soft upsell in both cases.

RECOMMENDATION: Buy Decodo. It is the stronger choice for our use case on three
counts:

  (1) Decodo states in its own official docs, verbatim, that "Google targets are
      accessible with rotating residential proxies" — that is positive, explicit
      permission, whereas IPRoyal's all-clear rests on Google's ABSENCE from
      blocked lists (negative evidence) plus a generic robots.txt AUP clause.

  (2) Decodo's pool is larger and independently verified higher quality (115M+
      IPs, 95.87% genuinely residential, ~99.8% global success), while IPRoyal
      posted only 66.77%-70% success on Google specifically, with up to 30%
      CAPTCHA in independent tests.

  (3) IPRoyal's KYC, while it doesn't gate Google, restricts you to ~40% of the
      pool until you submit a government ID + selfie — the closest thing to a
      Bright-Data-flavored gate in this comparison.

IPRoyal's only real edge is non-expiring traffic and slightly cheaper bulk
pricing. For a budget Google/AIO scraper that wants to avoid getting burned
again, Decodo is the safer, more explicitly-sanctioned bet — verify with the
$0 / 100 MB free trial first (plan in section 7).

3. IPROYAL — DETAIL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Google-SERP verdict: Permitted, but only "likely" rather than "confirmed" — the
all-clear rests on Google's absence from every blocked/KYC/restricted tier plus a
generic robots.txt AUP clause (sec 2.3.14 / 3.2), not an explicit vendor
statement. No bad_endpoint refusal mechanism exists in their error-code docs. The
catch is QUALITY, not policy: independent tests measured 66.77% Google success at
avg 5.97s (Proxyway) and ~70% with ~30% CAPTCHA (GoLogin) on the shared
residential pool. Distrust the 98.2% figure from proxylook.com (vendor-aligned).

Pros:
  - Google permitted on raw residential; markets residential explicitly for
    SERP/SEO scraping.
  - NON-EXPIRING traffic — purchased GB roll over until used (a genuine edge over
    both Bright Data and Decodo subscriptions).
  - Clean HTTPS passthrough, no cert install.
  - 7-day sticky sessions (longest in this comparison), unlimited concurrent
    sessions, country/state/city/ISP targeting at no extra cost.
  - Cheapest bulk: down to ~$1.75/GB at 1TB.
  - Zero monthly minimum.

Cons:
  - KYC wall touches the same nerve Bright Data did: gov-ID + selfie (via
    third-party iDenfy) required to unlock the full pool and the Residential API;
    without it you scrape from ~40% of IPs (~13.6M). Google works pre-KYC, but on
    the reduced pool.
  - Mediocre Google hit-rate (66-70%) -> must budget for retries/CAPTCHA handling.
  - Smaller real pool than headline: an independent 21-day crawl saw only
    ~535,859 unique IPs from the global gateway.
  - Weak/conflicting refund terms — treat paid traffic as non-refundable.
  - $10-spend threshold to become KYC-eligible; $500 to unlock extra domains
    (irrelevant to Google, but signals a gated culture).

Fit for our browser + warm-up architecture: Good on mechanics —
http://USER:PASS@geo.iproyal.com:12321 with geo/session flags embedded in
credentials, 7-day sticky sessions ideal for warming up an IP before hitting
Google, clean passthrough so Playwright/Firefox needs no ignoreHTTPSErrors. The
66-70% Google rate means our warm-up + retry logic will be doing real work; the
long sticky window helps amortize a warmed IP across many AIO queries.


4. DECODO (formerly Smartproxy) — DETAIL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Google-SERP verdict: Permitted — and this is the ONLY provider here with explicit,
positive documentation: "Google targets are accessible with rotating residential
proxies with 3GB, 10GB, 25GB, 50GB, 100GB plans and Pay As You Go." Google is
absent from both the KYC list (Mailing/Streaming/Business only) and the
permanent-block list (Banking/Government/Ticketing/Gaming/Telecom). The error-code
docs show only generic 403 (retry different IP) and 525 (no exit node) — no
bad_endpoint refusal. No Google-specific success rate is published anywhere, so
the headline 99.8% numbers are global/CDN benchmarks, not Google-proven.

Pros:
  - EXPLICIT written permission for Google on raw rotating residential — the
    single best mitigation of our #1 fear.
  - No blanket KYC, and NONE for Google; full pool from first dollar.
  - Clean HTTPS passthrough proven by their own Playwright example (no cert/SSL
    workaround).
  - Large, independently-verified pool: 115M+ IPs advertised, 95.87% genuinely
    residential, 466,068 unique US IPs found in a 14-day test; ~99.8-99.86%
    global success, ~0.63s response, ranked fastest of 17 providers (Apr 2025).
  - Flexible monthly subscriptions, no long-term lock-in, low entry
    (3GB = $11.25).

Cons:
  - PAYG is expensive at $8.50/GB (vs IPRoyal ~$7) — only worth it for tiny
    volumes; buy a subscription tier for real work.
  - Harder SERP-API cross-sell than IPRoyal, though it's optional.
  - Sticky sessions cap at 24h (vs IPRoyal's 7 days) — fine for warm-up, just
    shorter.
  - No published Google-specific success/CAPTCHA rate — you still own CAPTCHA
    handling at scale (true of any residential network).
  - Some user reports of ISP/static IPs flagged as datacenter — NOT relevant to
    rotating residential, which is what we want.

Fit for our browser + warm-up architecture: Strong. Single backconnect gateway
http://user:pass@gate.decodo.com:7000 (rotating) or sticky ports 10001+; sticky
sessions 10 min default up to 1440 min (24h) via -sessionduration-, geo via
-country-us-city-chicago. Clean passthrough means our Firefox/Playwright stack
runs unchanged (no cert, no ignoreHTTPSErrors). Caveat: long sticky sessions may
drop early if the underlying residential device goes offline — build
session-refresh into the warm-up loop.

6. HONEST RISKS & UNKNOWNS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Not verified / thin evidence — stated honestly:

  - No Google-specific success rate exists for Decodo. The 99.8%+ numbers are
    global/CDN benchmarks. Decodo's OWN guide concedes "Scraping Google can
    quickly turn frustrating when repeatedly met with CAPTCHA challenges." The
    trial must measure the Google-specific rate ourselves.

  - IPRoyal's "Google permitted" is inferred, not stated. It rests on Google's
    absence from blocked lists + a generic robots.txt AUP clause (sec 2.3.14/3.2).
    That clause, read aggressively, covers automated Google querying — but it's
    boilerplate present even in providers that openly sell SERP scraping, with no
    evidence of residential-side enforcement. Rated "likely," not "confirmed."

  - TLS-passthrough confidence is MEDIUM for IPRoyal (convergent silence across
    sources, not an explicit statement) and HIGH for Decodo (proven by their own
    cert-free Playwright example).

  - IPRoyal's separate Web Unblocker product was not verified and could behave
    differently (it's an anti-bot/MITM-style proxy). This finding applies only to
    raw rotating residential.

  - Exact $/GB varies by source/promo for IPRoyal ($3.50-$7.35 at 1 GB) — confirm
    live at checkout.

What could still go wrong regardless of provider:

  - CAPTCHA at scale. Google fraud-scores residential IPs; both networks will
    throw CAPTCHAs under load. IPRoyal showed this concretely (30% CAPTCHA in one
    test). Plan for retry + IP-rotation logic, not a clean 100%.

  - AI Overview non-determinism. AIO does not render for every query, every geo,
    or every session — it's gated by Google's own triggering logic and varies by
    location, query intent, and personalization. A "missing AIO" in results may
    be Google's behavior, not a proxy failure. Our warm-up/geo-targeting must be
    tuned to AIO-triggering query+geo combos, and our success metric must separate
    "page fetched" from "AIO present."

  - Sticky-session drops (Decodo, when the residential device goes offline) —
    build session-refresh into the warm-up loop.

Test-before-you-scale: never commit money on either before measuring our OWN
Google/AIO hit-rate on the free trial. The headline benchmarks won't predict our
number because they don't test Google specifically (Decodo) or test on a shared
pool with high fraud scores (IPRoyal).


7. FINAL RECOMMENDATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Buy Decodo. It is the only provider with explicit written permission for Google on
raw residential, has the larger and independently-verified-cleaner pool, carries
no KYC gate anywhere near our use case, and proves clean HTTPS passthrough with
its own cert-free Playwright example. Its weaknesses (pricey PAYG, soft SERP-API
push, 24h sticky cap) are minor and none is a Bright-Data-style blocker. IPRoyal
is a reasonable fallback — its non-expiring traffic + 7-day sticky sessions are
genuinely nice — but its 66-70% Google success rate and gov-ID KYC wall make it
the second choice for someone whose explicit goal is to NOT get burned again.

RUN THIS EXACT CHEAP TEST FIRST (~$0, ~1 hour) on the Decodo 3-day/100 MB free
trial — cancel before it converts:

  1. Sign up, take the free trial (cancel-anytime; the 14-day money-back doesn't
     apply to the trial, so just don't let it auto-convert).

  2. Point our existing Playwright/Firefox stack at
     http://USER:PASS@gate.decodo.com:7000 — confirm it runs with NO
     ignoreHTTPSErrors and NO CA cert (this directly proves the cert-error trap
     is gone).

  3. Validate Google access: fetch https://www.google.com/search?q=... through a
     rotating IP. Confirm real SERP HTML and NO bad_endpoint-style refusal (this
     proves the core Bright Data trap is gone).

  4. Measure real numbers over ~50-100 requests across target geos using
     -country-XX-city-YY: record (a) HTTP success rate, (b) CAPTCHA rate, and
     (c) AIO-PRESENT rate specifically (separate "page loaded" from "AI Overview
     rendered"). This is the number no benchmark gives us.

  5. Test a warmed sticky session: use a sticky port / -session-<id>-
     sessionduration-30, warm the IP, then hit the AIO queries — compare hit-rate
     vs cold rotating.

  6. Decision rule: if AIO-present + success rate is acceptable on the trial, buy
     the 3 GB subscription ($11.25, $3.75/GB) — NOT PAYG ($8.50/GB) — and scale up
     tiers as volume grows. If Decodo's CAPTCHA rate is too high, run the
     identical test on IPRoyal's trial before deciding, then weigh its cheaper
     bulk + non-expiring traffic against its lower Google hit-rate.

DO NOT pre-pay either provider before step 4 gives a real, Google-specific,
AIO-specific success number — that single measurement, not the marketing
benchmarks, is what protects us from a second burn.

A3. KEY INSIGHT — DOES CHEAPER = WORSE GOOGLE? IS THE SAVING REAL?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Yes. For Google specifically, cheaper reliably correlates with worse success, and
the headline saving largely evaporates once you account for retries/CAPTCHA waste.

(a) Cheap residential pools are P2P/recycled and reputation-tainted. The cheap
    tiers (Evomi Core via Earn.fm, Proxy-Cheap's pool, DataImpulse regular)
    recycle the same residential IPs across many customers. Google fraud-scores
    heavily-reused residential IPs harder than any other target on the web — so
    the exact thing that makes these pools cheap is what makes them fail on Google.
    Proxyway diagnosed Evomi's residential as having "seen more abuse than most
    competing providers" — note the Google-specific gap on the SAME pool: ~62%
    Google vs ~93% Amazon vs ~79% Instagram.

(b) The cheap sticker is NOT the cheap effective cost. At ~62% Google success
    (Evomi) you pay ~1.6 attempts per successful page (~38% retry overhead). Worse,
    Evomi's $0.49/GB applies ONLY with no advanced settings — enabling the
    fraud-score filtering / extra-IP / ASN targeting needed to lift Google yield
    multiplies bandwidth "up to 15x in extreme scenarios." The better Google
    numbers only appear on the PREMIUM pools (Evomi Premium ~$2.15-4, DataImpulse
    Premium $5) — at which point you're no longer "cheap" and the proven-Google
    providers (Oxylabs ~93%, Infatica ~94%, Decodo ~87%) become competitive.

(c) For a cheap provider, the ABSENCE of independent Google data is itself a risk
    signal. Proxy-Cheap has none and Proxyway leaves it out of its Google ranking;
    DataImpulse's cheap pool has none (only premium measured); Evomi's only
    independent Google number is mediocre. When a provider is cheap AND there's no
    neutral Google measurement, assume the silence is bad news — the providers that
    perform well on Google publish those numbers.
